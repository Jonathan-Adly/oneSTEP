Resources

Links to useful resources that I found useful during my journey.

Free Resources

  • FreeCodeCamp: A very straight-forward & accessible resource to start coding. Make sure to combine it with a formal class ( CS50 for example), so what you are doing doesn't seem like nonsensical magical incantations.
  • Edx.org: Real college courses from Harvard, MIT, and more of the world’s leading universities. Mostly Free. CS50 is an excellent first step.
  • Open Source Society University: Basically, a free quality CS degree! CS50 is an excellent first step.
  • W3 Schools: Tons of how-tos and tutorials.
  • Codepen.io: An online editor and a resource. With real developers sharing their code & how they built things for FREE. Make sure to thank them when they inspire you.
  • Repl.it: When you want to try out your code quickly and pain-free. Has underrated tutorials as well.
  • Bootstrap: The CSS library used to build this site (Library = An easy way to make sure your CSS code is decent without googling every little attribute). CS50 has a good intro to bootstrap & Bootstrap documentation and W3 resources will fill out the rest.

Almost Free Resources

  • CodeAcademy: Reasonably priced with more advanced modules. Decent amount of hand-holding. Beginner friendly.
  • Udemy: Varies widely depending on topic/class. Good if you know exactly what you want to learn and who is a good instructor. Not beginner friendly.
  • Coursera: Less variability than Udemy. Although you still have to know exatly what you want to learn and what to enroll in. You need to figure out the pre-requisites for courses on your own. Pricing is complicated and not transparent.
  • Udacity: Easier if you are not exactly sure what skills you need. Transparent prerequisites and steps needed to achieve goal.

Coding bootcamps

You can learn almost anything over the internet without paying $10,000+ in tuition fees. The value of bootcamps are not the materials, rather the network and the jobs. Quincy Larson, the founder of a FreeCodeCamp sums the pros and cons perfectly in this small handbook.

CoderrificAcademy is a company I founded to teach kids how to code. My own slight variation on the coding bootcamp theme. The strucure and organized classes is needed for most kids, at least initially. Once they get the fundmentals down, they too can learn anything on their own.
